Measure of cross-sectional Area of a wire Using Screw Gauge

Aim:

Measure of cross-sectional area of a given wire using Screw gauge.

Apparatus:

Screw gauge, given wire, magnifying glass or rexined.

Internal Jaws Locking Screw M5 Depth measuring Blade Vernier Calipers Clamping Lever External Jaws Stud Screw circular scale pitch scale Ratchet Thimble Sleeve (Screw Gauge)

Theory:

1) Description of Screw Gauge: Screw Gauge is a mechanical tool that facilitates measuring the diameters or radius of thickness of a thin wire or thickness of a thin metal sheet with utmost accuracy. This tool consists mainly of a U-Shaped frame and a spindle (or Screw) attached to the thimble. There are two scales known as pitch scale which is the main scale and is engraved on the barrel with vertical lines. Another one is the circular scale which is engraved horizontally on the thimble. Pitch scale measures the distance in millimeter (mm) travelled by the spindle per revolution. One revolution by the circular scale is equal to half of the millimeter of displacement of the screw is called a micrometer screw gauge. Micrometer gauges are more accurate and precise as compared to the vernier caliper scale.

2) Pitch of the screw: The pitch of the screw is the distance through which the screw moves along the main scale in one complete rotation of the cap on which is engraved the circular scale.
Pitch = Distance moved on the main scale / Number of complete rotations given

3) Least Count: The least count of the screw gauge is the distance through which the screw moves when the cap is rotated through one division on the circular scale.
Least count (Lc) = Pitch of the screw gauge / No. of divisions on circular scale

4) Back-lash Error: Owing to ill-fitting or wear between the screw and the nut, there is generally some space for the play of Screw. The screw may not move along its axis for appreciable rotation of the head (cap, on which circular scale is marked). The error so introduced is called back-lash error. To eliminate it, you must advance the screw, holding it by the ratchet cap, when making final adjustment for finding zero error of the wire.

5) Zero Error and Zero Correction: When the opposite studs on the frame are brought into contact, if the zero of the head scale coincides with the circular scale axis, there is no zero error. When the zero mark of the circular scale and the main scale do not coincide on bringing the studs in contact, the instrument has zero error. The Zero of the circular scale may be in advance or behind the zero of the main scale by a certain number of divisions on circular scale.

  • • Positive Zero Error: If the zero of the circular scale is behind the zero of the pitch scale (i.e., below the reference line), the Zero Error is positive. Correction is negative.
  • • Negative Zero Error: If the zero of the circular scale is ahead of the zero of the main scale (i.e., above the reference line), the zero error is negative. Correction is positive.

With the studs in contact, observe the number of divisions by which zero of the circular scale deviates from zero of the main scale. This number multiplied by the least count gives the required Zero Error. Zero correction is the negative of zero error and is added algebraically to the observed diameter of wire to get the corrected reading.
Zero correction = – (Zero Error)

6) Formula for Observed Diameter: To measure the diameter of the wire, move the screw back to make a gap between the studs. Insert the wire between the studs. Turn the screw forward by holding it from the ratchet cap and wire should be held gently between the two studs. Read the nearest division on the circular scale in the line with the main scale and also find the complete rotations of the cap with the help of the main scale.
Observed diameter = Pitch scale reading + (circular scale reading × L.C)

7) Formula for cross-sectional Area: If 'd' is the corrected diameter of the wire, its cross-sectional area (A) is given by A = (π d²) / 4

Observations:

(i) Pitch of the Screw Gauge:
Distance moved on the main scale for 4 rotations = 4 mm
Pitch = 4mm / 4 = 1 mm
Total number of divisions on the circular scale (CSD) = 100
Least count (L.C) = Pitch / CSD = 1 mm / 100 = 0.01 mm

(ii) Zero Error:
On bringing the studs into contact, the 85th division of the circular scale coincides with the reference line.
This is a Negative Zero Error because the zero of circular scale is ahead of the main scale zero.
Zero error (e) = – (100 – 85) × 0.01 = –0.15 mm
Zero correction (c) = – e = +0.15 mm

Calculations:

Mean observed Diameter = 0.575 mm
Corrected Diameter: Mean Observed Diameter + Zero Correction = 0.575 + (+0.15) mm = 0.725 mm
Hence, the true diameter of wire, d = 0.725 mm.

Cross-sectional Area (A):
A = (π d²) / 4 = (3.1416 × (0.725)²) / 4 = 1.651 / 4 ≈ 0.413 mm²

Result:

The diameter of the given wire is 0.725 mm.
Its cross-sectional area is 0.413 mm².

Precautions:

  • • The screw should be moved by using the ratchet to avoid applying undue pressure, which could damage the wire or the instrument.
  • • To eliminate back-lash error, always advance the screw forward (in the same direction) when making final adjustments.
  • • The zero error shall be noted carefully and applied with the correct sign.
  • • Measurement should be taken at different points along the wire and in perpendicular directions at each point to account for any non-uniformity.

📐 Measurement of Volume of a solid cylinder Using Vernier Callipers

1) Aim:

Measurement of volume of a solid cylinder using vernier calliper.

2) Apparatus:

Vernier Callipers, solid cylinder.

3) Theory:

(i) Description of vernier callipers: Slide callipers consists of a steel plate at one edge of which a centimeter scale (cm) is marked while at its other edge an inch scale is marked. At right angles to the steel Plate (P) there are two jaws (AA₁ and BB₂), one of which (AA₁) is fixed at one end of the Plate while the other jaw (BB₂) is provided with a vernier scale (V) and can slide over the main scale on the Plate (P). When the two jaws touch each other, the gap between them becomes zero and the zero-line of the vernier usually coincides with that of the scale. If it does not happen so, then there is a zero error in the instrument and this should be determined for correct result. Sometimes a narrow thin steel strip (T) is attached parallel to the plate which can move with the movable jaw BB₂. This is useful for depth measurement.

The instrument has two types of jaws:
• External Jaws (AA₁ and BB₂) used for measuring external diameters and lengths.
• Internal Jaws used for measuring internal diameters of hollow objects.

(ii) Vernier constant (vc) or Least Count (LC): At first the vernier constant is to be determined. The movable Jaw BB₁ is drawn outward to create a gap between the jaws. If we are to measure the diameter of a rod or a bob, it should be put between the jaws A and B. If we are to measure the internal diameter of a hollow cylinder, the jaws A and B are to be put into the hollow of a cylinder. The position of the sliding jaw is then adjusted till the jaws touch the object. Let the integral number of main divisions on the left side of the zero-line of vernier be S cm. If the v-th division of the vernier scale coincides with a main scale division, then the total length is:
Total Reading = S + (v × vc) cm

The vernier constant is the smallest measurement that can be made with the instrument. It is the difference between the value of the main scale division (1MSD) and one vernier scale division (1VSD).
Vc or LC = 1MSD – 1VSD
Often, the relationship between the scales is given, e.g., m VSD = (m–1) MSD. In such a case LC = 1MSD / m.

(iii) Zero Error: Two jaws of scale are in contact. If zero lines of main scale and vernier scale coincide, then zero error doesn't exist.

  • • Positive Error: If zero-line of vernier is on the right of zero-line of main scale: e = +y × vc (where y is the coinciding division). This is to be subtracted from the observed reading.
  • • Negative Error: If zero-line of vernier is on the left of zero-line of main scale: e = –y × vc. This is to be added to the observed reading.
  • • Corrected value: Corrected length = measured length – e.

Formula for volume of a solid cylinder: If 'D' is the external diameter and 'L' the length of the cylinder, its volume (V) is given by V = (π D² L) / 4.

Observations:

(i) Value of 1 main scale Division (1MSD): 0.1 cm
Relation between scales: 10 VSD = 9 MSD
1 VSD = 0.9 MSD = 0.9 mm = 0.09 cm
Vernier Constant/Least Count (LC): LC = 1MSD – 1VSD = 0.1 cm – 0.09 cm = 0.01 cm

(ii) Zero Error: On bringing the jaws in contact, the zero of the vernier scale coincides with the zero of the main scale.
Zero error, e = 0.00 cm.

Calculations:

Volume of the cylinder:
V = (π D² L) / 4 = (3.1416 × (2.468)² × 6.784) / 4 = 32.445 cm³

Result:

The volume of the given solid cylinder is approximately 32.445 cm³.

Precautions:

  • • The jaws should be gently pressed against the object; excessive force can lead to incorrect reading and damage the instrument.
  • • Ensure the cylinder is held firmly and squarely between the jaws.
