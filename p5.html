To Determine the Value of g by Bar Pendulum

The Bar Pendulum as a compound Pendulum

A bar Pendulum is a physical realization of a compound pendulum - any rigid body capable of oscillating freely about a horizontal axis passing through any point of the body, other than its center of gravity. Unlike a simple pendulum where all mass is concentrated at a point, the mass in a compound Pendulum is distributed along its length. This distribution introduces additional complexity and richness in its oscillatory behaviour.

Moment of Inertia & Parallel Axis Theorem

Consider a rigid bar of mass M and total length L. Let G be its center of gravity. When the bar oscillates about a horizontal axis through point at distance l from G, the moment of inertia about this axis given by the Parallel Axis Theorem:
I = I_g + ml²
where I_g is the moment of inertia about the center of gravity.
Expressing I_g in terms of radius of gyration k (where I_g = mk²): I = m(k² + l²)
The radius of gyration k represents the distance from the center of gravity at which the entire mass can be assumed to be concentrated to yield the same moment of inertia.

Derivation of Time Period

When the bar is displaced through a small angle, the restoring force is provided by the weight component...
τ = –mg l sinθ
For small angles, τ = –mglθ
From rotational dynamics τ = Iα = I(d²θ/dt²)
Equating: I(d²θ/dt²) = –mglθ
Substituting I = m(k² + l²):
d²θ/dt² + gl/(k² + l²) θ = 0

This is the differential equation of SHM with angular frequency ω = √[gl / (k² + l²)].
Therefore T = 2π √[(k² + l²) / gl]

Analysis of Time Period T vs l

Variation with l: The time period T varies with distance l from center of gravity. As l changes, T first decreases, reaches a minimum, and then increases.
Condition for minimum: Differentiating with respect to l and setting dT/dl = 0 yields: l = k.
At this point, the minimum time period is T_min = 2π √(2k / g)
This provides an alternative method to determine k experimentally.

Equivalent Length of Simple Pendulum

For any suspension distance l on one side of center of gravity there exists another distance l' on the opposite side giving the same time period. These are called conjugate points. The distance between such conjugate points gives the equivalent length L of a simple pendulum: L = l₁ + l₂.
Substitute into Simple Pendulum formula:
T = 2π √(L / g) This allows determination of g without knowing k.

Precautions for Bar Pendulum Experiment:

  • The knife edge must be perfectly horizontal and sharp to minimize frictional losses at the pivot point.
  • The bar should oscillate in the vertical plane without touching any adjacent objects or experiencing drafts.
  • Amplitude of oscillations should be small (less than 5 degrees) to maintain the small-angle approximation (sinθ ≈ θ).
  • Time measurements should be recorded for a large number of oscillations (20 or more) to minimize the percentage error in Time Period calculation.
  • The center of gravity must be accurately located by balancing the bar on the knife edge before beginning measurements.

Sources of Error:

  • Air resistance and friction at the knife edge can slightly dampen oscillations.
  • Parallax error while noting the time or reading the scale.
  • Human reaction time in starting/stopping the stopwatch.
  • Inaccurate location of center of gravity.
  • Large amplitude oscillations violating SHM conditions.
