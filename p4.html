To Determine the moment of Inertia of (a) A cylindrical (b) A Rectangular Bar About an axis Passing Through Its Centre of Mass

1) Aim:

To determine the moment of Inertia (M.I.) of:
(i) A given cylindrical body
(ii) A given rectangular bar
About an axis that passes exactly through their Center of Mass (C.O.M.) Using a Torsional Pendulum (Dynamic Method).

2) Apparatus: Name of Apparatus - Purpose

Torsional Pendulum Standard Cylinder Unknown Cylinder Rectangular Bar Stopwatch Vernier Calipers Physical Balance
  • (i) Torsional Pendulum (Cradle Type) - To hold the bodies and allow torsional oscillations. It consists of a circular frame (Cradle) suspended by a long, thin wire clamped at the top.
  • (ii) Standard Cylinder - A Cylinder of known mass and radius used as a reference to calculate the known moment of Inertia (I₁).
  • (iii) Unknown cylindrical Body - The test body whose M.I. is to be determined.
  • (iv) Unknown Rectangular Bar - The test body whose M.I. is to be determined.
  • (v) Stopwatch - To measure the time taken for a fixed number of oscillations (e.g., 20 oscillations).
  • (vi) Vernier Slide Calipers - To measure the radius (R) of the standard cylinder accurately.
  • (vii) Physical Balance - To measure the mass (M) of the standard cylinder.

3) Theory and Principle:

3.1 Basic Principle
The experiment works on the principle of Torsional oscillation. When the cradle is rotated slightly in the horizontal plane from the rest position and released, it executes torsional simple harmonic motion (SHM) due to the restoring torque produced in the suspension wire. The time period (T) of this oscillation is directly related to the Moment of Inertia (I) of the system.
The relation is given by T = 2π √(I/C)
where:
• T = Time period of oscillation (seconds)
• I = Moment of Inertia of the oscillating system (kg.m²)
• C = Torsional Constant of the suspension wire (depends on material and dimensions).
Since the torsional constant (C) of the wire is unknown and remains constant throughout the experiment, we use the comparison method to eliminate it.

3.2 The "Axis through centre of Mass" condition:
For the Moment of Inertia to be calculated correctly about a specific axis, that axis must physically pass through the center of Mass of the body.
In this experiment:
• The suspension wire defines the axis of rotation (vertical axis).
• The body placed on the cradle must be positioned such that its Center of mass lies exactly on this vertical line (i.e., directly under the wire).

3.3 Working Formula
By comparing the time periods of the empty cradle, cradle with known body, the cradle with unknown body, one can eliminate the Torsional constant and find the unknown M.I.
I₂ = I₁ × [ (T₂² – T₀²) / (T₁² – T₀²) ]
where:
• I₁ = moment of Inertia of the Standard cylinder about its central axis (Calculated directly from its mass and radius).
• I₂ = moment of Inertia of the Unknown body (Cylinder or Bar) about an axis through its C.G. (Parallel to the axis of rotation).
• T₀ = Time period of the empty Cradle (seconds).
• T₁ = Time period of the cradle + standard cylinder (seconds).
• T₂ = Time Period of the cradle + Unknown Body (seconds).

3.4 Calculation of I₁ (standard cylinder):
For a solid cylinder rotating about its central longitudinal axis, the M.I. is given by I₁ = (½) M R²
where:
• M = Mass of the standard cylinder (kg)
• R = Radius of the standard cylinder (m)

5. Observation and Data:

5.1 Data for Standard cylinder (Calculation of I₁)

  • • Mass of standard cylinder – M = 0.678 kg
  • • Radius of standard cylinder – R = 0.0164 m
  • M.I. of Standard Cylinder (I₁) = (1/2) × 0.678 × (0.0164)² = 9.12 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²

6. Calculations:

6.1 For the Unknown cylindrical Body:
Given:
• I₁ = 9.12 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²
• T₁² = 8.12 s²
• T₀² = 7.13 s²
• T₂² (for cylinder) = 7.56 s²
Using the formula:
I_cyl = (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × [ (7.56 – 7.13) / (8.12 – 7.13) ]
= (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × (0.43 / 0.99)
= (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × 0.4343
= 3.96 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²

6.2 For Rectangular Bar:
Given:
• I₁ = 9.12 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²
• T₁² = 8.12 s²
• T₀² = 7.13 s²
• T₂² (for Bar) = 7.78 s²
Using the formula:
I_bar = I₁ × [ (T₂² – T₀²) / (T₁² – T₀²) ]
= (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × [ (7.78 – 7.13) / (8.12 – 7.13) ]
= (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × (0.65 / 0.99)
= (9.12 × 10⁻⁵) × 0.6566
= 5.99 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²

7. Result:

The moment of Inertia of the given bodies about an axis passing through their Center of mass is found to be:
(a) Cylindrical Body = 3.96 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²
(b) Rectangular Bar = 5.99 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²

8. Discussion:

Axis through Center of mass: The success of this experiment heavily depends on accurately placing the bodies so that their center of mass lies exactly on the axis of rotation (the suspension wire). Any misalignment would introduce error as the body would not rotate purely about its C.G.
Oscillation Type: By keeping the initial angular displacement small, we ensured that the oscillations remained within the elastic limit of the wire, satisfying the conditions for Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM).
Accuracy: Timing for 20 oscillations (instead of just 1) significantly reduced the percentage error in measuring the time period.

9. Precautions and Sources of Error:

Precautions:

  • (i) Centering (Most Important): Ensure that the geometric center of the body coincides exactly with the center of the cradle so that the axis of rotation passes as precisely through the C.O.M.
  • (ii) Small Amplitude: Always keep the initial angle of twist small (less than 5°). Large amplitudes may violate the elastic limit and SHM conditions.
  • (iii) Repeatability: Take at least three readings for each time period and use the mean value for calculation.

Sources of Error:

  • • Air Resistance: Although minimal, air drag can slightly dampen the oscillations over time.
  • • Parallax Error: While using the stopwatch or measuring instruments.
  • • Friction: Friction at the clamp or within the wire can slightly affect the time period.
  • • Misalignment: If the body is not perfectly centered, the effective M.I. measured will be different from the true M.I. about C.O.M.

10. Conclusion:

The moment of Inertia of the unknown cylindrical body and the rectangular bar about axes passing through their respective centres of mass was successfully determined using the torsional pendulum method.
The obtained values are:
• Cylinder: 3.96 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²
• Rectangular Bar: 5.99 × 10⁻⁵ kg.m²
